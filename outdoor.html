Outdoor

Offering a spectacular range of outdoor activities along a beautiful landscape New Zealand is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you're into water-rafting, hiking, or thrill-seeking adventures, New Zealand has something for all. This is a list of the best adventures to embark upon during your stay:

The Tongariro Alpine

The Tongariro Alpine Crossing is one of New Zealand's most iconic and popular day hikes, renowned for its dramatic volcanic landscapes, emerald lakes, and challenging terrain. Located within the Tongariro National Park on the North Island, this trek is often considered one of the world's best one-day hikes.

Trail Details:

Duration: The hike typically takes 6-8 hours to complete, depending on your pace and stops along the way.
Trailhead The trek starts at Mangatepopo car park and ends at Ketetahi car park. Most people hike from Mangatepopo to Ketetahi, as it's considered the easier direction.
Season: The best time to hike the Tongariro Alpine Crossing is during the summer months (December to April) when the weather is milder. Winter conditions can be extremely challenging and are only recommended for experienced hikers.

Lake Taupõ

Lake Taupō is the largest lake in New Zealand and one of the most prominent natural attractions on the North Island. It is renowned for its stunning beauty, recreational opportunities, and geothermal wonders.

What to See and Do:

Scenic Cruises: Take a cruise on Lake Taupō to appreciate its vastness and stunning surroundings. Scenic boat tours offer unique perspectives of the lake and its geological features.
Fishing: Lake Taupō is famous for its trout fishing, particularly rainbow and brown trout. Anglers from around the world visit to try their luck.
Water Sports: The lake is ideal for various water sports, including swimming, kayaking, paddleboarding, and water skiing
Geothermal Hot Pools: On the lake's eastern shores, near Taupō town, you can relax in geothermal hot pools and enjoy therapeutic mineral waters.
Trout Hatcheries: Visit the Tongariro National Trout Centre to learn about trout conservation and even feed some fish.
Hiking and Biking: The surrounding area offers numerous walking and cycling trails, with options for all fitness levels. The Great Lake Walkway is a popular choice.

Mount Ngauruhoe

Mount Ngauruhoe, often referred to as "Mount Doom" from J.R.R. Tolkien's "The Lord of the Rings" film trilogy, is an iconic volcano located in the Tongariro National Park on New Zealand's North Island.

Mount Ngauruhoe, with its unique appearance and cultural significance in the "Lord of the Rings" movies, is a captivating natural landmark in New Zealand. Whether you choose to hike it or simply admire it from afar, it remains a symbol of the country's volcanic heritage and natural beauty.

Geothermal hot pools

Geothermal hot pools can be found in various regions across the country, offering visitors a unique opportunity to soak in warm, mineral-rich waters while surrounded by stunning landscapes.

Tips for Visiting Geothermal Hot Pools:

⦁ Follow any rules and guidelines provided by the hot pool operators to protect both visitors and the environment.
⦁ Be prepared for varying water temperatures, as some pools can be quite hot.
⦁ Consider visiting at different times of the day to experience the pools in different lighting conditions.
⦁ Some hot pools may offer spa and wellness services for added relaxation.

Visiting geothermal hot pools in New Zealand is not only a great way to unwind but also an opportunity to connect with the country's unique geothermal heritage while surrounded by its breathtaking natural beauty.

The Waitangi Treaty Grounds

The Waitangi Treaty Grounds, located in the Bay of Islands on the North Island of New Zealand, hold immense historical and cultural significance. This is where the Treaty of Waitangi was signed on February 6, 1840, between representatives of the British Crown and Māori chiefs, marking the beginning of New Zealand as a nation.

Visiting Tips:

⦁ Allow several hours to explore the grounds and the various exhibits.
⦁ Plan your visit to coincide with a cultural performance to fully experience Māori culture.
⦁Consider joining a guided tour to gain a deeper understanding of the historical and cultural context of the site.

White-water rafting on the Kaituna River

White-water rafting on the Kaituna River is an exhilarating and adrenaline-pumping adventure in the Bay of Plenty region on New Zealand's North Island. This river is famous for its thrilling white-water rapids and the opportunity to conquer the world's highest commercially rafted waterfall.

Visiting Tips:

⦁ Wear comfortable swimwear to go under your wetsuit.
⦁ Bring a towel and change of clothes for after the adventure.
⦁ Listen carefully to your guide's instructions for a safe and enjoyable experience.
⦁ Rafting trips are available year-round, but water levels can vary depending on the season.
