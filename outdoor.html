Outdoor

Offering a spectacular range of outdoor activities along a beautiful landscape New Zealand is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you're into water-rafting, hiking, or thrill-seeking adventures, New Zealand has something for all. This is a list of the best adventures to embark upon during your stay:

The Tongariro Alpine

The Tongariro Alpine Crossing is one of New Zealand's most iconic and popular day hikes, renowned for its dramatic volcanic landscapes, emerald lakes, and challenging terrain. Located within the Tongariro National Park on the North Island, this trek is often considered one of the world's best one-day hikes.

Trail Details:

Duration: The hike typically takes 6-8 hours to complete, depending on your pace and stops along the way.
Trailhead The trek starts at Mangatepopo car park and ends at Ketetahi car park. Most people hike from Mangatepopo to Ketetahi, as it's considered the easier direction.
Season: The best time to hike the Tongariro Alpine Crossing is during the summer months (December to April) when the weather is milder. Winter conditions can be extremely challenging and are only recommended for experienced hikers.

Lake Taupõ

Lake Taupō is the largest lake in New Zealand and one of the most prominent natural attractions on the North Island. It is renowned for its stunning beauty, recreational opportunities, and geothermal wonders.

What to See and Do:

Scenic Cruises: Take a cruise on Lake Taupō to appreciate its vastness and stunning surroundings. Scenic boat tours offer unique perspectives of the lake and its geological features.
Fishing: Lake Taupō is famous for its trout fishing, particularly rainbow and brown trout. Anglers from around the world visit to try their luck.
Water Sports: The lake is ideal for various water sports, including swimming, kayaking, paddleboarding, and water skiing
Geothermal Hot Pools: On the lake's eastern shores, near Taupō town, you can relax in geothermal hot pools and enjoy therapeutic mineral waters.
Trout Hatcheries: Visit the Tongariro National Trout Centre to learn about trout conservation and even feed some fish.
Hiking and Biking: The surrounding area offers numerous walking and cycling trails, with options for all fitness levels. The Great Lake Walkway is a popular choice.
